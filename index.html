It’s November, and we are a busy shop. Pretty much like every year at this time of year. 
 
Our agency is full of opportunities, with several clients asking for big ideas. And some high-profile companies knocking on our door, asking us to pitch. What could be better?
 
While all of this is what we want, it’s important to focus on doing the things that clients need from us consistently: proactive thinking. It’s easy to get caught up in being busy executing (“doing”), and good execution is vital. But it cannot distract from thinking ahead, and bringing business-building ideas.
 
When clients are polled about what they need most from agencies, proactive ideas typically tops the list. And when BG responds accordingly, we hear about it. Our clients love it. And they value us even more.
 
Something to think about while we are busy ‘making’ the next few weeks.

Auto Dealers Association of Greater Philadelphia (ADAGP) — The Auto Dealers Association of Greater Philadelphia is wrapping up its month of giving. This year, through its Driving Away the Cold program, ADAGP distributed 52,700 brand-new winter coats to children in need in the 5-county region. To kick off the month, on October 8th at the Philadelphia Convention Center, more than 8,000 brand-new winter coats were distributed to 1,000 students from the Philadelphia School District. For the PCC distribution event, we secured a total of 20 broadcast hits from FOX 29 (Good Day Philadelphia), 6ABC, KYW, NBC10, and CBS3. Dranoff — What came first: 450 tons of rebar or 16 million pounds of concrete? October was a crucial month for Arthaus, marking a key construction milestone. On October 12, over 400 truckloads of concrete were poured into the Arthaus foundation over a period of eight hours, with the assistance of more than 250 workers. Now, the building goes up!
 
In celebration of the milestone, a week later, Carl Dranoff and team hosted a Construction Carnival – a free, family friendly event for the neighborhood. Attendees were able to get close to the action on the job site, learn about design and construction, and have an opportunity to “meet the makers” who have personally been involved with the project. Families were able to participate in fun, interactive activities such as: touch-a-truck, build-your-own tower, and more!
 
The BG PR team was instrumental in securing media coverage for the concrete pour; generating buzz and awareness of the Construction Carnival, and landing Carl Dranoff a live interview on Good Day Philadelphia.

NJM — After months of planning, the PR team successfully executed NJM’s Teen Driver Safety Week kick-off event on October 21 at the Wells Fargo Center. With help from the Philadelphia 76ers, PA DUI, Brain Injury Alliance of New Jersey (BIANJ), and inspirational speaker Gabe Hurley, 193 students from 12 different high schools in PA and NJ participated in the event focusing on teen driver safety. From a Sixers pep rally to navigating a PA DUI Safety Bug obstacle course and everything in between, the students were engaged throughout the event and walked away with experiences and knowledge that are sure to empower them down the road.  Thanks to a robust media outreach plan, the BG team earned 15 media placements for our client, including hits on 6abc, NBC10, CBS3, msn.com, yahoo.com and others for the event along with 14 social media posts on both media and the attending schools’ channels. Overall, the client was extremely pleased with the results and is looking forward to building on the work done in 2019 for an even bigger event next year!
